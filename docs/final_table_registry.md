# Table Guide

The tables are organized around three tasks: estimating responses, comparing persistence, and checking whether the interpretation survives alternative samples and measures.

## Response Tables

- Normalized impulse responses.
- Cumulative responses.
- Peak timing and peak magnitude.
- Horizon-level significance.
- Stability and persistence summaries.

## Identification And Proxy Tables

- First-stage relevance screens.
- Information-effect event screens.
- Accepted and rejected monthly proxy candidates.
- Compensation-proxy rankings.

## Mechanism And Robustness Tables

- Banking and lending-condition timing.
- Sequential transmission checks.
- Rolling and recursive stability.
- Regime comparisons.
- HAC and bootstrap uncertainty.
