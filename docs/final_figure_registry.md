# Figure Guide

The figures are meant to support the thesis argument, not to document every intermediate step.

## Main IRFs

Baseline impulse-response figures show how each monthly response variable moves after an ECB easing surprise.

## Persistence Figures

Cumulative-response and persistence figures show whether movements last beyond the impact horizon. These are the most useful figures for the housing-credit versus compensation-linked comparison.

## Uncertainty Figures

Fan charts, interval comparisons, and significance maps show where the response profile is stable enough to interpret.

## Robustness Figures

Rolling, recursive, clean-event, and exclusion figures check whether the main hierarchy depends on one sample period or event group.

## Mechanism Figures

Banking and sequential-timing figures show whether lending rates, spreads, and credit variables move before or alongside housing-credit responses.

## Proxy Figures

Proxy-ranking figures explain why the monthly comparison uses housing-credit and wage-pressure measures rather than interpolated house-price or compensation series.
