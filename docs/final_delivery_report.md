# Reading The Repository

The project is organized for thesis review rather than software review. The main notebook presents the research design, figures, tables, and interpretation in one place.

## What To Read First

- The notebook for the empirical walkthrough.
- The methodology note for the identification and response design.
- The findings note for the main result.
- The claim-boundary note before writing causal language.

## What The Outputs Show

The results compare responses across housing credit, lending conditions, credit aggregates, financial markets, wages, and compensation-linked proxies. The central pattern is stronger persistence in house-purchase lending growth and lending spreads than in wage-related channels.

## What To Ignore

Archive material, old experiments, and implementation details are not needed for the thesis argument. They preserve research history but should not drive the final interpretation.
