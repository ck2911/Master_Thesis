# Causal Interpretation

## Identified Object

The thesis identifies monthly responses to high-frequency ECB monetary-policy surprises. The shock is measured around policy announcements and then used to trace how financial, housing, credit, and compensation variables move afterward.

## What The Design Supports

- Dynamic responses to ECB policy-news surprises.
- Persistence comparisons across financial, housing-finance, and compensation channels.
- Timing evidence from lending rates, spreads, credit stocks, and banking variables.
- A comparative claim that asset-side and housing-finance responses are more durable than wage-pressure responses.

## What It Does Not Support

- Welfare or inequality effects.
- Exact redistribution magnitudes.
- Direct monthly house-price effects.
- Direct monthly compensation-per-employee effects.
- Structural bank-mediation estimates.
- Fully identified regime-specific treatment effects.

## Working Claim

Expansionary ECB surprises transmit more persistently through financial and housing-finance channels than through compensation-related wage-pressure proxies.
