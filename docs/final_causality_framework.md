# Causal Interpretation

## Identified Object

The thesis identifies monthly responses to high-frequency ECB monetary-policy surprises. The shock is measured around policy announcements and then used to trace how housing credit, lending conditions, credit aggregates, financial markets, and compensation-linked variables move afterward.

## What The Design Supports

- Dynamic responses to ECB policy-news surprises.
- Persistence comparisons across housing-credit, intermediation, financial-market, and compensation-linked channels.
- Timing evidence from lending rates, spreads, credit stocks, and banking variables.
- A comparative claim that house-purchase lending growth and lending spreads are more durable than compensation-linked proxies.
- An information-effect interpretation of mixed equity-market responses.

## What It Does Not Support

- Welfare or inequality effects.
- Redistribution magnitudes.
- Direct monthly house-price effects.
- Direct monthly compensation-per-employee effects.
- Structural bank-mediation estimates.
- Fully identified regime-specific treatment effects.

## Working Claim

Expansionary ECB surprises transmit most clearly and persistently through housing-credit and selected financial-intermediation channels, while compensation-linked real-economy evidence is weaker and less consistent.
