# Thesis Outline

## Motivation

Post-COVID ECB policy raised a distributional transmission question: did easing mainly support asset and housing channels, or did it pass through strongly into household income?

## Research Question

Do expansionary ECB monetary-policy surprises transmit more persistently through financial and housing-finance channels than through compensation-pressure channels?

## Identification

The shock is a high-frequency ECB announcement surprise. Monthly aggregation keeps the timing advantage while allowing comparison with monthly macro-financial variables.

## Data

The analysis uses financial variables, lending spreads, credit quantities, housing-finance proxies, wage-pressure proxies, and compensation-related indicators. Lower-frequency house-price and compensation series are not interpolated.

## Econometrics

Monthly impulse responses compare channel dynamics at short, medium, and longer horizons. Cumulative responses are used for persistence comparisons.

## Results

Financial and housing-finance responses are more durable than compensation responses. Banking variables support a financial-intermediation reading.

## Robustness

Alternative shocks, clean events, rolling estimates, recursive estimates, exclusions, and alternative proxies preserve the main hierarchy.

## Boundary

The thesis identifies reduced-form transmission to policy-news shocks. It does not identify welfare, inequality, redistribution, exact house-price effects, or exact compensation effects.
