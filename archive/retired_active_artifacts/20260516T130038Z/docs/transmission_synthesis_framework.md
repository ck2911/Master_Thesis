# Transmission Synthesis Framework

This document defines the structure for the final economic synthesis after empirical stabilization. It is a framework, not final thesis interpretation.

## A. Liquidity Transmission

Evidence to summarize:

- ECB asset response: sign, timing, peak, cumulative response, and weak-IV status
- Credit responses: household loans and NFC loans separately
- Housing response: peak, persistence, cumulative response
- Compensation response: timing, persistence, cumulative response

Required discipline:

- report first-stage relevance alongside every structural pattern
- separate statistical significance from economic magnitude
- distinguish point-estimate direction from robust structural evidence
- avoid interpreting weak long-horizon tails as settled dynamics

## B. Financial Versus Real Channels

Core comparison:

`credit + housing transmission`

against:

`compensation transmission`

The synthesis may discuss:

- whether housing responses are more persistent than compensation responses
- whether credit responses align more with household or firm intermediation
- whether compensation responses are delayed, small, or unstable relative to financial variables

The synthesis must not claim:

- welfare deterioration
- inequality causality
- household affordability causality
- political conclusions about central banking

## C. Regime Mutation

Regime evidence should be organized around the frozen regime windows:

| Regime | Start | End |
|---|---:|---:|
| pre-QE | 2005Q1 | 2014Q1 |
| QE | 2014Q2 | 2019Q4 |
| COVID | 2020Q1 | 2021Q4 |
| tightening | 2022Q1 | 2025Q4 |

Core questions:

- Did QE amplify housing transmission?
- Did compensation weaken relative to asset-price and credit channels?
- Did household lending dominate NFC lending?
- Did COVID concentrate identification strength or distort transmission estimates?

Required discipline:

- state when regime estimates are interaction-based rather than separately estimated within a short subsample
- flag regimes with limited observations
- keep tightening interpretation separate from the baseline sample when evidence requires the 2025Q4 robustness window

## D. Asymmetry

The asymmetry section should compare easing and tightening dynamics only after the robustness sample is stabilized.

Allowed interpretation:

- directional asymmetry in credit, housing, or compensation responses
- differences in timing, persistence, and cumulative response
- differences in relevance diagnostics across regimes

Not allowed:

- counterfactual paths
- historical decompositions
- FEVD-style statements
- policy recommendations

## Final Synthesis Rule

The thesis identifies macro-financial propagation of ECB liquidity shocks. The final synthesis should remain about the evolving structure of liquidity transmission across balance sheets, credit, housing, and compensation.

It should not become a normative account of whether central banking is good or bad.
