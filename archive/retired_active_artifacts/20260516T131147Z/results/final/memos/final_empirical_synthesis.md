# Final Empirical Synthesis

ECB liquidity transmission appears more persistently visible in housing and macro-financial balance-sheet channels than in compensation dynamics under weak-identification constraints.

The final result is not a precise magnitude estimate. Anderson-Rubin inversion leaves many cells unbounded, so the thesis-facing inference rests on repeated directional stability across LP-IV, OLS comparison, cumulative responses, regime interactions, and robustness layers.

Housing and household-credit channels carry the strongest persistence signals. Compensation is less stable and remains more fragile across horizons and regimes.
